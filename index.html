Sachin Tendulkar

The man of a billion dreams

Here's a timeline of Sachin Tendulkar's life

  • 1973 - Born in Mumbai
  • 1988 - Frist class debut
  • 1989 - International debut
  • 1990 - Scores his maiden Test century (119 not out, 17 fours) against England at Old Trafford and helps India to save the match.
  • 1992 - Plays a match winning innings of an unbeaten 54 against Pakistan at Sydney in the 1992 World Cup. Also takes the crucial wicket of Aamir Sohail and ends up with the Man Of The Match award.
  • 1994 - Scores maiden ODI ton in a match against Austrailia
  • 1996 - Scores a total of 523 runs in the World Cup hosted by India, Pakistan and Sri Lanka which includes two centuries.
  • 1997 - Leads India to a 4-1 victory over Pakistan at Toronto in the second edition of the Sahara Cup. Wins the Wisden cricketer of the year award.
  • 2001 - Completes 10K runs in ODIs
  • 2002 - Surpasses Bradman's record of 29 test centuries
  • 2003 - Makes 673 runs in 11 matches in the ICC World Cup - most by any batsman in a single World Cup
  • 2004 - Equals Gavaskar's record of 34 Test tons
  • 2009 - Becomes the first batsman to aggregate 17,000 ODI runs during his innings of 175 against Australia at Hyderabad.
  • 2010 - Becomes the first batsman to score a double-century, brings up the feat in ODI vs South Africa at Gwalior.
  • 2011 - Hits two hundreds and is first to score 2000 runs in World Cups as India win the World Cup beating Sri Lanka in the final at Wankhede.
  • 2012 - Scores his 100th international century in an Asia Cup match against Bangladesh, the first player ever to do so.
  • 2013 - Retires from all forms of international cricket at his home ground
